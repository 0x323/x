1. Introduction

Lotto Chain is a decentralized smart contract platform built on blockchain technology, designed to support digital lotteries, gamification incentives, and decentralized financial services. The brand, logo, and source code of Lotto Chain are core assets representing the platform’s identity, trust, and technological innovation. This policy governs the global protection, usage, and enforcement of these assets, independent of governmental jurisdictions, to ensure integrity, consistency, and economic value within the decentralized ecosystem.

Objectives

  1. Protect the brand, logo, and source code as intellectual property worldwide.
  2. Regulate usage by the community, developers, and partners to maintain platform reputation and functionality.
  3. Provide a fair, transparent, and decentralized dispute resolution mechanism.
  4. Promote innovation and adoption through a strong brand identity and protected code.

2. Definitions

In this Policy, the following terms are defined:

  1. Lotto Chain Brand: A graphical mark, including the name “Lotto Chain,” logo, color scheme (blue #2b6cb0 and white #ffffff), slogans, or other visual elements distinguishing the platform’s services in the blockchain ecosystem.
  2. Lotto Chain Logo: The official graphic design of the platform, combining text, imagery, and colors, serving as the primary visual identity.
  3. Lotto Chain Source Code: All software code, including smart contracts, decentralized applications (dApps), user interfaces, and technical documentation, developed for the platform, protected as a copyrighted work.
  4. Asset Owner: The decentralized entity (Lotto Chain DAO) holding exclusive rights to the brand, logo, and source code, as determined by community governance.
  5. User: Any individual, entity, or developer interacting with the platform, including those using smart contracts, dApps, or related services.
  6. Asset License: Permission granted via smart contract or written agreement by the Asset Owner to use the brand, logo, or source code within a specified scope.
  7. Asset Violation: Unauthorized use of the brand, logo, or source code, including copying, modification, or distribution harmful to the platform.
  8. Copyright: Exclusive rights over the logo (as artwork) and source code (as literary/technical work), recognized automatically under the Berne Convention 1886.
  9. Lotto Chain DAO: The decentralized autonomous organization managing platform governance, including asset-related decisions.
  10. License Smart Contract: A blockchain-based smart contract governing the issuance, management, and termination of asset licenses.
  11. Decentralized Arbitration: A dispute resolution mechanism via platforms like Kleros or Aragon Court for handling asset violations.

3. Scope & Principles

3.1 Scope

This Policy applies to:

  • Use of the Lotto Chain brand, logo, and source code in digital platforms, blockchains, and NFT markets.
  • Protection of assets in the global ecosystem, including decentralized finance and digital lotteries.
  • Management of licenses via smart contracts or written agreements.
  • Enforcement of sanctions and dispute resolution through decentralized mechanisms.
  • Protection of the logo and source code as copyrighted works under international law.

3.2 Principles of Protection

  • Decentralization: Assets are managed via DAO and smart contracts, independent of centralized authorities.
  • Transparency: Decisions, licenses, and sanctions are recorded on the blockchain for public audit.
  • Integrity: Assets must be used in alignment with Lotto Chain’s mission (innovation, trust, gamification).
  • Absolute Rights: The Asset Owner holds exclusive rights, with the source code protected as absolute copyright.
  • Global Compatibility: Aligned with the Paris Convention, TRIPS Agreement, Madrid Protocol, and Berne Convention.

4. Asset Protection

4.1 Protection Status

  1. Lotto Chain Brand:
    • Protected as a distinguishing mark under first-to-use (evidenced on blockchain) and first-to-file (if registered via WIPO).
    • Includes the name “Lotto Chain,” typographic variations, and related visual elements.
  2. Lotto Chain Logo:
    • Protected as part of the brand and as artwork under the Berne Convention.
    • Includes 2D/3D designs, colors (blue #2b6cb0, white #ffffff), and animations (if applicable).
  3. Lotto Chain Source Code:
    • Protected as a literary/technical work under the Berne Convention, with absolute copyright owned by the Asset Owner.
    • Includes smart contracts (Solidity), dApps (JavaScript, React), backend, and technical documentation.
    • Prohibited from copying, modification, or distribution without explicit license.
  4. Ownership proof is recorded on the blockchain (e.g., code hash on IPFS, smart contract deployment timestamp).

4.2 Brand Registration

  1. The Asset Owner may register the brand via the Madrid Protocol through WIPO for protection in over 130 countries.
  2. Registration is optional, as first-to-use protection is recognized in blockchain ecosystems.
  3. Registration costs are covered by the DAO, funded by the Lotto Chain token treasury.
  4. Source code is not registered but protected automatically as copyright upon creation.

4.3 Rights of the Asset Owner

  1. Use the brand, logo, and source code for platform operations.
  2. Grant licenses via smart contracts or written agreements.
  3. Prohibit unauthorized use through DAO governance.
  4. Initiate disputes via decentralized arbitration.
  5. Receive royalties from licenses, managed via smart contracts.

5. Asset Usage

5.1 Permitted Usage

  1. Brand and Logo:
    • Used by the Asset Owner or authorized licensees.
    • Examples: Logo on dApps, “Lotto Chain” in whitepapers, branding in NFTs.
    • Must include ™ (unregistered) or ® (registered) symbols.
  2. Source Code:
    • Restricted to the Asset Owner or explicit licensees.
    • Limited to platform operations or licensed projects.
    • Examples: Official smart contract deployment, dApp integration by partners.
  3. Non-commercial community use (e.g., tutorials) is allowed with attribution: “© Lotto Chain, all rights reserved.”

5.2 Asset Licensing

  1. License Types:
    • Brand and Logo: For branding (e.g., partner dApps).
    • Source Code: Limited licenses for code use or modification (e.g., forks for licensed projects).
  2. License Mechanisms:
    • License Smart Contract: Automates rights, duration, and royalties.
      Example smart contract function for granting a license.
      function grantLicense(address user, uint256 duration, bytes32 assetType) public onlyDAO {
    licenses[user] = License(user, assetType, block.timestamp, duration);
    emit LicenseGranted(user, assetType, duration);
}
    • Written Agreement: For strategic partners, digitally signed and hashed on the blockchain.
  3. License Terms:
    • Scope: Virtual regions, service types (e.g., lotteries, DeFi).
    • Duration: E.g., 1 year, renewable via DAO.
    • Royalties: 5-10% of revenue, paid in Lotto Chain tokens or stablecoins.
    • Prohibitions: Modifying logo/code without approval, use for illegal purposes.
  4. License Revocation: Automatic via smart contract for:
    • Failure to pay royalties.
    • Violation of guidelines (e.g., altering logo colors).
    • Harm to platform reputation.

5.3 Usage Guidelines

  1. Brand and Logo:
    • Colors: Blue (#2b6cb0), white (#ffffff).
    • Resolution: 300 dpi (digital), 1200 dpi (print).
    • Prohibited: Altering design, adding elements, use in fraudulent contexts.
  2. Source Code:
    • Must follow official documentation (e.g., smart contract ABI).
    • Modifications only for licensees, with DAO approval.
    • Attribution required: “Source code © Lotto Chain”.
  3. Full guidelines are accessible via IPFS or the official Lotto Chain website.

5.4 Non-Commercial Use

  1. Community may use brand/logo for education (e.g., blogs, videos) with attribution.
  2. Source code is not permitted for non-commercial use unless open-sourced under a specific license (e.g., MIT, approved by DAO).
  3. Harmful non-commercial use (e.g., FUD, phishing) is considered a violation.

6. Violations & Sanctions

6.1 Types of Violations

  1. Brand:
    • Unauthorized use of “Lotto Chain” name.
    • Use of similar marks (e.g., “LottoChainX”).
  2. Logo:
    • Copying or modifying logo for other projects.
    • Distributing logo in NFTs without permission.
  3. Source Code:
    • Copying smart contracts/dApps without a license.
    • Modifying code for fraudulent purposes (e.g., scam contracts).
    • Distributing code without attribution.
  4. Reputation: Using assets for fraud, phishing, or illegal activities.

6.2 Enforcement Mechanisms

  1. Warning: DAO issues a warning via blockchain, email, or Discord, with a 7-day period to cease violations.
  2. Smart Contract Sanctions:
    • Block violator’s access to dApps/smart contracts.
    • Seize related tokens (e.g., staking rewards).
    • Disable license via revokeLicense(address user).
      Example smart contract function for revoking a license.
      function revokeLicense(address user) public onlyDAO {
    delete licenses[user];
    emit LicenseRevoked(user);
}
  3. Decentralized Arbitration: Disputes are handled by Kleros or Aragon Court, with on-chain rulings.
  4. Violator Registry: Offenders are listed on a public blockchain registry, impacting their on-chain reputation.

6.3 Contractual Sanctions

  1. Fines:
    • Brand/Logo: 10,000-50,000 Lotto Chain tokens or USD 5,000-25,000.
    • Source Code: 50,000-200,000 tokens or USD 25,000-100,000, based on damage.
  2. License Revocation: Automatic via smart contract.
  3. Blacklist: Ban from the Lotto Chain ecosystem.
  4. Damages: Compensation for material (e.g., lost revenue) and immaterial (e.g., reputational) losses.

6.4 Copyright Sanctions (Logo and Source Code)

  1. Logo copying: Fine up to 100,000 tokens or USD 50,000.
  2. Source code copying: Fine up to 500,000 tokens or USD 250,000, plus cessation of distribution.
  3. Offenders must destroy digital copies (e.g., NFTs, code repositories).

7. Dispute Resolution

7.1 Mechanisms

  1. Community Mediation: Conducted via DAO forums with a neutral facilitator.
  2. Decentralized Arbitration: Handled by Kleros or Aragon Court, with binding on-chain rulings.
  3. Conventional Courts (Optional): If arbitration is infeasible, parties may use a neutral jurisdiction (e.g., Singapore), with DAO approval.

7.2 Costs

  1. Arbitration costs are borne by the losing party, paid in Lotto Chain tokens or USDC.
  2. Estimated: 1,000-5,000 tokens per case, depending on complexity.

7.3 On-Chain Evidence

  1. Evidence (e.g., code hashes, screenshots, transactions) is stored on IPFS or blockchain.
  2. DAO verification tools validate evidence authenticity.

8. Governance & Updates

8.1 Governance

  1. The Lotto Chain DAO manages:
    • License approvals.
    • Guideline updates.
    • Violation handling.
  2. On-chain voting requires a 51% token holder quorum for routine decisions, 66% for policy changes.

8.2 Policy Updates

  1. Proposed via DAO, approved with 66% token holder votes.
  2. Updates announced on Discord, Twitter, and the official website, with hashes on the blockchain.
