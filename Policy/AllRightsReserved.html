1. Background

The statement "Powered By Lotto Chain 2025 All Rights Reserved" appears in the footer of digital platforms, applications, websites, HTML emails, or marketing materials developed by Lotto Chain. Its purpose is to:

  • Affirm Lotto Chain’s intellectual property rights over its content, technology, code, design, and brand.

  • Inform global users that all elements associated with Lotto Chain are protected under international law.

This notice aligns with global intellectual property standards, particularly the Berne Convention for the Protection of Literary and Artistic Works (1886, as amended in 1979).

2. Meaning of the Statement

  • "Powered By Lotto Chain": Indicates that the platform, technology, or service was created, supported, or managed by Lotto Chain—the entity responsible for its development and operations.
  • "2025": Refers to the year the work was created, updated, or published, serving as a reference for copyright duration.
  • "All Rights Reserved": Declares that all copyrights, trademarks, and other intellectual property rights are exclusively held by the creator or rights holder. Unauthorized use, reproduction, modification, or distribution is prohibited.

3. Global Legal Framework

This statement is supported by the following international legal frameworks:

  • Berne Convention (1886, amended 1979):
    • Article 5(2): Copyright is automatically granted without formal registration in member countries.
    • Article 7: Copyright extends for a minimum of 50 years after the creator’s death (or longer in some jurisdictions, e.g., 70 years in the U.S. and EU).
    • Article 8: Covers literary, artistic, and technological works, including software, source code, and user interfaces.
  • TRIPS Agreement (1994):
    • Sets minimum standards for copyright and trademark protection among WTO members.
    • Articles 9–14: Provide protection for software, digital designs, and brands such as “Lotto Chain.”
  • WIPO Copyright Treaty (1996):
    • Recognizes software, computer code, and databases as protected works.
    • Provides safeguards against unauthorized copying or distribution in digital environments.
  • Common Law Practices: In jurisdictions such as the U.S., U.K., and Commonwealth countries, “All Rights Reserved” is recognized as a formal copyright notice, even though it is not required under the Berne Convention.
